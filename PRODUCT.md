# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

DFW-area homeowners and event hosts — Keller, Southlake, Colleyville, Fort Worth, and the surrounding 15-mile delivery radius — who need party and event equipment (tables, chairs, linens, and similar) for a single upcoming event. They arrive already knowing roughly what they need and want to move straight from browsing into a booked, paid order without picking up the phone.

## Product Purpose

DW Event Co is a party and event equipment rental business that delivers within a 15-mile radius of its Fort Worth base and does not offer pickup. The site's job is to replace the traditional call-for-a-quote rental workflow with a fully self-serve one: pick an event date, see live availability and pricing for that date range, add items to a cart, and check out online. Success right now is completed online bookings — the site should actively pull people away from defaulting to a phone call or text.

## Positioning

Instant online quote and booking — pick a date, see live availability and pricing immediately, and book and pay online in minutes, with no phone call required to get a straight answer.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Book Now / Get a Quote
- Secondary fallback: Browse Rentals (the product catalog), for visitors not ready to commit to a date yet
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Party rentals delivered to your door, booked online in minutes."
- Belief ladder: this is a real, reliable local business → they carry the right equipment for my event → delivery and pricing are simple and can be trusted at face value.
- Proof on hand: none yet. Lean on transparent pricing, a clearly stated service area, and professional presentation as trust signals until testimonials, reviews, or event photos are available.

## Brand Personality

Reliable, friendly, local. The site should read as a real DFW small business a homeowner can trust to show up on time and deliver what was promised — not a faceless national rental chain, and not styled like a slick SaaS product.

## Anti-references

No stylistic anti-references were named. The one hard constraint: **dweventco.com is the existing live site**, and its established flow — hero with date picker → browse/quote → checkout — should be preserved, not reinvented. Treat drifting from that flow as the thing to avoid more than any particular visual style.

## Design Principles

- **Replace the phone call, don't just decorate the page.** Every screen should move someone closer to a completed, self-serve booking; polish that doesn't reduce booking friction is secondary.
- **Real business, not a rental chain.** Trustworthy, local warmth wins over generic SaaS polish — avoid making the site feel like a faceless marketplace.
- **Preserve the live flow.** This build inherits the working flow of the existing dweventco.com; changes should refine that path, not restructure it, unless there's a specific reason to.
- **Show the trust signals you actually have.** Until testimonials or photos exist, lean on transparent pricing and a clearly stated service area rather than manufacturing social proof.
- **One event, one clear path.** A visitor already knows roughly what they need — keep the date → availability → price → book path short and obvious rather than branching them into exploration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard good practice: solid contrast, keyboard navigation, and reduced-motion support. No formal WCAG compliance level requested.
